**Analysis of Sentences for Ambiguity and Nuance**

1. **"I saw the man with the telescope."**
   - **Ambiguity:** The sentence is straightforward in its structure but can be interpreted differently based on context. While it's clear that "saw" is an active verb requiring a direct object, there isn't another inherent ambiguity unless considering potential sarcasm or double entendre.
   - **Possible Interpretations:**
     1. The speaker observed the man through a telescope (common interpretation).
     2. Sarcasms could imply that the man was unimpressive when viewed through a telescope.

2. **"The chicken is ready to eat."**
   - **Ambiguity:** This sentence can be ambiguous due to idiomatic usage, specifically the common phrase where "ready" implies something has been cooked once but can still be used again.
   - **Possible Scenarios:**
     1. The chicken was fully cooked and now waiting to be eaten (literal meaning).
     2. The chicken was poached once and is ready for another cooking cycle, implying it's partially cooked.

3. **"After months of practice, the pianist finally made her parents proud."**
   - **Implication:** This statement implies that the pianist's dedication and effort were evident during their practice period.
   - **Nuance or Subtext:**
     1. The implication is that the pianist put in significant effort and time to achieve this, showcasing skill and perseverance.

Each sentence was analyzed for potential layers of meaning beyond literal interpretation, considering both straightforward contexts and idiomatic expressions where applicable.

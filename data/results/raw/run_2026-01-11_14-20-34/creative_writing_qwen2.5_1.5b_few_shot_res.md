The clock struck midnight, but time had stopped moving three hours ago. The room was as quiet as a library on a Monday morning, save for the ticking of a nearby alarm clock that hadn't been set to go off until noon.

Lucas sat at his desk, staring blankly ahead. His mind wandered back through the endless paperwork and meetings that made up his day. He glanced around the cluttered office: piles of files, scattered papers, and an unopened letter from his mother requesting a family visit. The air in the room was thick with the scent of coffee and distant incense.

Suddenly, Lucas’s phone buzzed on its charger. His heart skipped a beat as he picked it up. It was from his ex-girlfriend, Sarah, whom he hadn't spoken to since they broke up two years ago. She asked if he could meet her at a local cafe for drinks.

Lucas hesitated, unsure of what he should do. He could either ignore the text and continue with the day, or jump into action and meet his ex-girlfriend in the middle of the night. The thought of Sarah's words—_"I'm sorry we can't be together anymore"_—flooded him like a cold wind.

The clock struck again, and Lucas realized he had to make a decision. He looked at his watch: 12:30 AM. Time was running out, and the clock seemed to be moving backward before his eyes. His mind raced through the options: keep working, meet Sarah, or just sit here until dawn broke.

Lucas’s phone buzzed again, this time from his mother. She asked if he could come home early for dinner—Sarah's suggestion had been a relief on their last date night together. Lucas sighed and decided to go with the plan. He glanced around the room one last time before standing up and walking towards the door.

As he crossed the threshold, he felt a strange sense of anticipation and urgency. The clock struck again, now closer to midnight. Was there something more in that final hour? Or was it just another moment in Lucas's life that had been cut short by circumstance?

He stepped outside into the cool night air, feeling the chill creeping up his spine. He walked towards Sarah’s café with a mix of nervousness and excitement. The clock struck once more as he entered, signaling the beginning of what would be an unexpected journey.

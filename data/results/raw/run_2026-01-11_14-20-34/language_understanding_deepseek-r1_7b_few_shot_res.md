**Analysis of Sentences for Ambiguity and Nuance**

1. **"I saw the man with the telescope."**
   - **Ambiguity:** The phrase "with the telescope" can be interpreted in two ways:
     1. As an adjective modifying "man," indicating that the man was using his telescope.
     2. As a prepositional object, suggesting that the man was being seen through the telescope (implying he saw something else).
   - **Most Likely Interpretation:** The first interpretation is more plausible as it implies the man used his own or another's telescope.

2. **"The chicken is ready to eat."**
   - **Ambiguity:** "Ready to eat" can mean:
     1. The chicken is alive and merely waiting to be consumed.
     2. The chicken has been prepared for eating, but may still be alive or dead.
   - **Most Likely Interpretation:** This refers to a chicken that is alive and in a state ready for consumption.

3. **"After months of practice, the pianist finally made her parents proud."**
   - **Nuance:** The sentence implies a significant achievement:
     1. It suggests the pianist achieved something important during extensive practice.
     2. The effort indicated is leading to a notable accomplishment that made her parents proud.
   - **Implied Nuance:** This highlights the pianist's dedication and success, suggesting she exceeded expectations through her practice.

Each sentence presents either ambiguity or nuance based on context:
- The first sentence leans towards a specific action with an implied owner.
- The second sentence clarifies the chicken's state based on typical usage of "ready."
- The third sentence emphasizes achievement and effort through practice.
